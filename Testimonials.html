Over the last few decades, our society has undergone revolutionary changes in technology, industry, and politics, but women are still fighting for their rights. People's misogynistic thinking desperately consumes the privileges of women. Not only has modern society failed to serve established women's rights, but it has made it much more difficult for women to survive because of their safety. Violence against Pakistani women is constantly increasing. Pakistan is ranked 153rd out of 156 countries in the World Economic Forum's Global Gender Gap Index 2021. In South Asia, it ranks 7th out of 8 countries. The COVID19 embargo increased domestic violence by 25 percent. Pakistan received nearly 10,000 complaints of violence and abuse against women this year in Sindh alone, according to local media reports. According to Geographic News in Hyderabad, Pakistan, a total of 6,325 cases of women's rights infringement have been recorded since 2018.
